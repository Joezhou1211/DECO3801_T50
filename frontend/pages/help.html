Manage Your Account

Blaze Insight is a secure platform designed exclusively for government personnel. Access to the platform is controlled through a strict authentication process to ensure compliance with privacy and ethical standards.

Account Access

Only authorized government personnel can log in to Blaze Insight. This restriction is essential because the platform allows users to upload and manage sensitive data, including official government documents and datasets. Permissions are granted through the distribution of unique access tokens. If you are a government employee and need access to the platform, please contact us to request your token. Our team will verify your credentials to confirm your government status before issuing the token, ensuring that all access remains secure and fully compliant with privacy regulations and ethical guidelines.

My Details in Account Settings

In the Settings section of Blaze Insight, you can manage your personal information under My Details:

Name: You can update your display name to reflect any changes or preferences.

Password: For security purposes, you can change your password at any time. We recommend updating it regularly to maintain account safety.

Manage Your Details →

Security User ID: This is a unique identifier tied to your account, used for authentication and verification purposes. For security reasons, this identifier is immutable and cannot be changed.

Should you have any questions or need assistance with managing your account, please contact our support team.

Searching for Data

Blaze Insight provides “Data” section for viewing and exploring all datasets uploaded to the platform. You can easily access the data by navigating to the Data option in the navigation bar. Once there, you’ll be able to filter, explore, and search through a wide variety of datasets related to Australian bushfires, social media insights, and fake news detection, among others.

The Search page allows users to narrow down the datasets by using multiple filtering options to refine their search. Here’s a breakdown of how to use each feature on the search page:

Time Range

You can use the date pickers to set a custom range for data analysis. Selecting a date range will immediately filter the datasets to show only data from your selected time frame.

Location

You can filter datasets based on location by selecting specific regions, countries, or even states/provinces.A counter will show how many news or tweets are available for the selected location.

Topic

Use the topic filter to select from predefined categories, such as Wildlife and Environmental Impact, Climate Change Debate, or Government Response. This feature allows users to explore datasets centered around particular themes for in-depth analysis.

Sentiment

Filter data based on sentiment scores (positive, neutral, or negative). Adjust the slider to include only data that falls within a specific sentiment range.

Influence Factors

Influence Tweet Factor & Influence User: You can refine your data based on how influential the tweets or users are. Adjust the sliders to focus on highly influential content or users.

Verified Account

Choose to display only tweets from verified accounts, helping you filter out unverified or potentially unreliable data.

Interaction Counts

Retweet, Reply, Quote, and Favourite Counts: These filters allow you to narrow your results based on the interaction level of tweets. Whether you want to see highly retweeted content or those with more replies, these sliders will help you target the most engaged content.

Extended Entities (Images Count)

This filter allows you to focus on tweets that include images or other extended entities, giving you insight into how visual content may have influenced public discourse.

Keyword Filters

Author Keynode, and Hashtag Keynode: These dropdowns allow users to refine the search by filtering based on specific keywords, authors, or hashtags related to the data. This can help you focus on key contributors or major discussion points in the dataset.

Viewing and Downloading Data

Once your filters are set, you’ll be able to see a summary of the data that matches your criteria. This page gives you a snapshot of how many datasets, tweets, or news articles are available based on your selection.

Download Data

After refining your search, you can download the filtered datasets for further analysis or reporting purposes.

Using the Map page

The map page, accessible through the Map option in the navigation bar,provides users with three different viewing modes: World Map, Continent Map, and Country Map. Upon entering the page, the default view will be the World Map, displaying tweet data for each region. You can easily navigate through the options to explore specific continents or countries to analyze tweet distribution across different locations.

World Map

On the World Map, you can see the tweet volumes visualized through the intensity of color. The darker the color, the more tweets have been posted in that area. The right sidebar will show the global tweet count, fake news count, and the percentage of fake news among the total tweets.

Continent Map

When switching to the Continent Map, you can zoom into one continent and view its data in greater detail. Similar to the World Map, the right sidebar will display the tweet count, fake news statistics, and the top fake news topics for that continent. If country-specific data is available for the selected continent, it will appear in the sidebar as well.

Country Map

In the Country Map, the tweet count and fake news statistics for a specific country or region will be displayed. The sidebar shows the tweet volumes, fake news count, and the top fake news topics for the selected country. If you zoom into a specific state or province within the country, the statistics will adjust to show relevant data for that area.

Timeline:

All map views feature an automatic timeline that spans from November 8, 2019, to February 28, 2020. The timeline automatically plays, showing data for each date, or you can manually scrub through the timeline to view specific days. This allows users to track how tweet volumes change over time for each region.

Filter

At the top of the map, you can find a filter option where you can select between Global View or specific Continents and Countries. This lets you zoom in and out to see data from different areas more clearly.

Popup Information

On the bottom-left of the map, a pop-up box displays aggregated data for regions with unknown region_code. This box gives an overview of tweets and related statistics for regions that cannot be clearly identified.

Download Data

Users can download filtered data by date to analyze offline. This feature allows you to select specific regions and export tweet, fake news, and other data for deeper analysis.

Right Sidebar

The right sidebar displays a summary of key statistics for different areas, including Total Tweet Count, Fake News Count, and the Fake News Percentage along with the top fake news topics. If specific country data is available, these will also be listed in the sidebar.

Analyse Map →

Using Topic page

The Topic page, accessible through the Topic option in the navigation bar, offers two distinct ways to explore data related to the Australian bushfires: Chart View and Table View. Both views allow users to analyze topics over time, with filtering and download options for further investigation.

Timeline:

Both views come with a timeline feature that allows users to automatically play a daily accumulation of data, starting from November 8, 2019, and continuing through each subsequent day until January 24, 2020. The subtitle dynamically updates to display the current date range, such as "from 8 Nov 2019 to ...", as the data plays.

Users can also manually drag the timeline slider to explore data for any specific day, with the selected date clearly reflected in the subtitle. Only dates between November 8, 2019, and January 24, 2020, are available for selection.

The timeline helps visualize how topics and sentiment trends evolve over time.

Quick Date Selection:

At the top of both views, users can easily select specific dates for analysis without needing to drag the timeline slider manually.

Download:

The filtered data can be downloaded for further analysis by clicking the Download button.

Chart View

The chart displays 10 key topics related to the Australian bushfires. Users can adjust the timeline to view data for each day, including the total number of tweets, sentiment visualization (with colors representing positive, neutral, and negative emotions), and totol number of fake news tweets.

Filtering:
The filter allows users to select specific sentiments (positive, neutral, or negative), and only the corresponding data will be displayed. Users can also view cumulative data over a custom date range to see how topics and sentiments evolve over time.

Table View

Detailed Topic Data:
Like the chart, the table displays the same 10 topics. However, it provides additional details beyond the total number of tweets, including the number of replies, shares, likes, and quotes for each topic. The table also shows the percentage breakdown of positive, neutral, and negative sentiments for each day, along with the number of fake news tweets.

Filtering:
In Table View, users can filter by date range, similar to Chart View, but without the option to filter by sentiment.

Analyse Topics →

Using the Fake News Detector

Fake News detection helps identify misinformation. This tool allows users to analyze news and tweets to determine their reliability.

Detecting Fake News

1. Selecting News:
To check the authenticity of a news article, select the News option from the dropdown menu and paste the article's text into the provided field.

2. Analyzing the News:
After pasting the content, click the Analyze button. The tool will evaluate several factors, including semantic similarity, time consistency, location consistency, and numeric consistency. Based on this analysis, the tool provides a probability score indicating the likelihood that the news is fake.

Detecting Fake Tweets

1. Selecting Tweet:
To check the authenticity of a tweet, select the Tweet option from the dropdown menu. You can either paste the tweet's text into the provided field:

or directly upload a tweet file by clicking the Upload Tweet button:

2. Analyzing the Tweet:
After pasting or uploading the tweet, click the Analyze button. The tool will conduct the same analysis process as it does for news articles, evaluating semantic similarity, time, location, and numeric consistency. The results will provide a probability score indicating whether the tweet is likely fake.
