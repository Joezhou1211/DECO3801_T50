Searching for Data

Blaze Insight provides a“data” for viewing and exploring all datasets uploaded to the website. You can easily access the data by navigating to the "Data" section found in the navigation bar on every page. From there, you can browse through various datasets relevant to your interests, making it simple to discover information that meets your research or analytical needs.

The "Data" section is designed to be intuitive, allowing users to efficiently filter and sort datasets based on different criteria, such as topic, date, or data source. Once you've located a dataset that interests you, you can view detailed metadata about the dataset, including its description, source, and any associated documentation that helps in understanding the data.

Using the Map

The map shows real-time data on geographical locations and hotspots for bushfires or other environmental concerns. Users can navigate through the map to see specific areas affected.

The bottom-left part of the map shows unknown regions of the data, including tweets, fake news, and their ratio.

The top-left part of the map has a "Download Data" button that allows you to download the whole dataset.

Using Topic

Topic Modeling allows users to analyze and extract key themes or topics from a set of data such as news articles or tweets. This helps in understanding patterns and trends in a dataset.

The example shows whether a topic is positive or negative, and includes a timeline to filter data by time.

Using the Fake News Detector

Fake News detection helps identify misinformation. This tool allows users to analyze news and tweets to determine their reliability.
